In the preparation of the timetable above, the Chair was guided by the following considerations: (a) In accordance with established procedures and practices and bearing in mind the availability of documentation, the Committee may wish to hold one joint general debate covering agenda items 54 to 58 and individual debates on agenda items 46 to 53, 59, 121 and 137, on the understanding that draft resolutions and/or decisions will be considered separately; (b) Representatives of Non-Self-Governing Territories and petitioners will be heard as early as possible during the Committee’s consideration of the related items, and the Chair suggests that the Committee devote its meetings from Tuesday, 7 October to Monday, 13 October, to the hearing of petitioners; (c) Item 121, Revitalization of the work of the General Assembly, is allocated to all the Main Committees for the sole purpose of considering and taking action on their respective tentative programme of work; A/C.4/80/L.1 25-14603 8/8 (d) Item 137, Programme planning, is allocated to all the Main Committees and the plenary of the General Assembly to enhance the discussion of evaluation, planning, budgeting and monitoring reports. If the Assembly allocates any report submitted under this item to the Fourth Committee, the Committee shall revert to this item.

Subject to the provisional programme of work above being acceptable to the Committee and in order to facilitate its work, the Chair wishes to suggest that the list of speakers in the general debate for the respective agenda items be closed as indicated in the timetable above.

7

The Chair also wishes to draw the attention of members of the Committee to the decisions of the General Assembly on the organization of its work contained in document A/80/250, sections II.B to III.

8

Members are also requested to bear in mind the decision of the General Assembly that morning and afternoon meetings should start promptly at 10 a.m. and 3 p.m., respectively, both for plenary meetings and for meetings of the Main Committees during the eightieth session. The attention of the members is also drawn to the decision of the Assembly to waive, at its eightieth session, the quorum required, under rule 108 of the rules of procedure of the General Assembly, for the Chair to declare a meeting open and permit the debate to proceed (see A/80/250, para. 8).

9

In view of the limited number of meetings available to the Committee, it is suggested that the time limit for statements be set at 10 minutes, with the exception of statements made by the spokespersons of groups of delegations, which would be extended to 15 minutes. In this connection, the Chair will set the time limit for the petitioners as deemed necessary.

10

The list of documents submitted under the various agenda items allocated to the Special Political and Decolonization Committee (Fourth Committee) is contained in document A/C.4/80/INF.1, which will be updated as necessary.
