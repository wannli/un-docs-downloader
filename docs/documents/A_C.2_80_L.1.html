In accordance with General Assembly decision 78/534 on the revitalization of the work of the Second Committee and following consultations with the membership of the Committee, the Bureau proposed the theme “Five years to 2030: multilateral solutions for sustainable development” for the general debate, taking into account the theme agreed by the Assembly for the 2026 session of the Economic and Social Council and the 2026 high-level political forum on sustainable development. process
5

Delegations may make only one national statement during the debate. The statement may cover any or all items allocated to the Committee. General discussions of agenda items, introduction of reports and question time

6

During the general discussions of agenda items, the introductions of the reports of the Secretary-General will be made through brief oral statements of no more than six minutes at the meeting at which the item is considered. Some flexibility with regard to the time limit may be exercised when two or more reports are introduced in one statement. Immediately following the introduction of reports, and in accordance with the practice of the Main Committees, delegations will be invited to make comments or pose questions to the presenters on the reports introduced. Delegations are invited to participate actively in the dialogues with questions and unscripted comments. Such interventions should be brief and to the point, not exceeding two minutes. Statements by delegations

7

The lists of speakers for the general debate and the general discussions of individual agenda items will be opened on 16 September 2025. For planning purposes, inscriptions in the general debate should be submitted by 29 September and in the general discussions by 1 October. In accordance with General Assembly decision 65/530, statements in the general debate should be limited to no more than 10 minutes for statements made on behalf of groups and 7 minutes for national statements. Statements in the general discussions of items should be limited to no more than five minutes for statements made on behalf of groups and three minutes for national statements. The lists of speakers for a given meeting will be prepared on the basis of these time limits, which will be strictly implemented, including, as necessary, by switching off microphones automatically after the allotted time. Pursuant to General Assembly resolution 72/313, delegations are encouraged to adhere to the “all protocol observed” principle and to refrain from listing standard protocol expressions during their statements. In the same resolution, the Assembly recommended that speakers should be mindful of the speed of statement delivery, bearing in mind the need for accuracy of interpretation.

8

In accordance with previous decisions of the General Assembly and the practice of the Committee, delegations should exercise their right of reply at the end of the day whenever two meetings have been scheduled for the consideration of the relevant agenda item on that day, or at the conclusion of the consideration of the item, if that is sooner. The number of interventions in exercise of the right of reply for any delegation at a given meeting should be limited to two per item, with the first intervention limited to five minutes and the second, if any, to three minutes.

9

Also in accordance with previous decisions of the General Assembly and the practice of the Committee, explanations of vote should be limited to five minutes, and points of order should be limited to three minutes. Conference facilities and meeting arrangements

PGA
10

Unless otherwise specified, in-person meetings of the Second Committee will be held at 10 a.m. and 3 p.m. in Conference Room 2. The General Assembly has stressed that punctuality is of the utmost importance in the interest of ensuring an effective and orderly organization of work and achieving economies for the United Nations. In this regard, rule 108 of the rules of procedure of the Assembly requiring the presence of at least one quarter of the members of the Committee to declare a meeting open and permit the debate to proceed has been waived by the Assembly. 11

In accordance with established practice, informal consultations will be used for expressing general observations on the proposals and for agreeing on them ad referendum. All drafting will be done at the level of informal informal consultations (without interpretation or other services) and will be conducted by the appointed facilitators. The Bureau will coordinate a comprehensive calendar of informal consultations, within regular meeting hours, while limiting parallel negotiations to the extent possible. process
12

Pursuant to paragraph 4 (c) of section D of the annex to General Assembly resolution 58/316, the item entitled “Report of the Economic and Social Council” should be considered in its entirety in plenary; subsequently, it was clarified by the General Committee that the relevant parts of chapter I of the report that fall under agenda items already allocated to the Main Committees would be considered by the Committee concerned for final action by the Assembly (see A/59/250/Add.1, para. 4). Conclusion of the work of the Committee

13

Bearing in mind the number of meetings available to the Second Committee, a target date of 26 November 2025 has been set for the conclusion of its work.
