Concerning agenda item 76, entitled “Responsibility of States for internationally wrongful acts”, the General Assembly, by its resolution 77/97, decided to include the item in the provisional agenda of its eightieth session and to further examine, within the framework of a working group of the Sixth Committee and with a view to taking a decision, the question of a convention on responsibility of States for internationally wrongful acts or other appropriate action on the basis of the articles on responsibility of States for internationally wrongful acts. In the light of that, three meetings, to be held on 15 October and 3 and 7 November, have been suggested for that purpose.

9

Concerning agenda item 109, entitled “Measures to eliminate international terrorism”, the General Assembly, by its resolution 79/129, decided to recommend that the Sixth Committee, at the eightieth session of the Assembly, establish a working group with a view to finalizing the process on the draft comprehensive convention on international terrorism as well as discussions on the item included in its agenda by Assembly resolution 54/110 concerning the question of convening a high-level conference under the auspices of the United Nations. In the light of that, two meetings, to be held on 17 October and 6 November, have been suggested for that purpose.

Concerning agenda item 86, entitled “Protection of persons in the event of disasters”, the General Assembly, by its resolution 79/128, decided that the Sixth Committee shall resume its session for up to five days by the end of April 2026 at the latest, in order to prepare a consolidated text that will serve as the basis for the negotiations of a legally binding instrument on the protection of persons in the event of disasters, including the draft articles on the protection of persons in the event of disasters and the proposals submitted by Governments, within the framework of a working group of the Committee. It is expected that the Sixth Committee will include, in the forthcoming draft resolution to be prepared under the agenda item, an indication of the dates and number of meetings of the working group. Statements by delegations

13

The list of speakers for the debate on all agenda items opened on 15 September

14

Pursuant to General Assembly resolution 72/313, delegations are encouraged to adhere to the “all protocol observed” principle and to refrain from listing standard protocol expressions during their statements. In the same resolution, the Assembly recommended that speakers should be mindful of the speed of statement delivery, bearing in mind the need for accuracy of interpretation. The delivery of national statements will be limited to 7 minutes, and group statements to 12 minutes, for all agenda items except those dealing with the annual report of the International Law Commission, for which delegations are encouraged to limit their statements to main positions and views for a total of 12 minutes (with further information or clarifications provided in written versions made available through the eStatements portal or in written submissions to the Commission).

15

In accordance with General Assembly decision 34/401, delegations should exercise their right of reply at the end of the day whenever two meetings have been scheduled for the consideration of the relevant agenda item on that day, or at the conclusion of the consideration of the item, if that is sooner. The first statement should be limited to five minutes and the second, if any, to three minutes. Draft resolutions

16

The initiation and sponsorship of draft resolutions, where applicable, will be undertaken electronically through the eSponsorship module for the Sixth Committee in the e-deleGATE portal.

17

Unless otherwise agreed, coordinators or main sponsors, as applicable, of proposals for draft resolutions are encouraged to hold at least one informal consultation per proposal. The Secretariat will provide assistance where necessary. Informal consultations should be announced sufficiently in advance, including in the Journal of the United Nations, so as to allow participation by representatives of all Member States. Once consultations have concluded, it is the practice of the Sixth Committee to place proposals for draft resolutions under a silence procedure, prior to their submission for processing and issuance in all official languages as limited (“L”) documents.

process
18

All delegations wishing to sponsor a proposal for a draft resolution, where applicable, are encouraged to do so electronically through the eSponsorship module, rather than during the formal proceedings of the Committee. This is without prejudice to the possibility of delegations sponsoring a proposal for a draft resolution at the time of the Committee’s action on the latter during a plenary meeting. Sponsorship should take place at the level of the Sixth Committee, given that, once the Committee has adopted a proposal for a draft resolution and recommended its adoption by the General Assembly, its sponsorship can no longer be altered by Member States. Proposals for draft resolutions submitted on behalf of the Bureau are not subject to sponsorship.

Each coordinator or main sponsor, as applicable, of proposals for draft resolutions will be invited to introduce their respective draft resolutions as issued in limited distribution (“L”) documents. 21

Except for draft resolutions introduced at the final meeting of the substantive part of the session on 21 November 2025, it is the practice of the Committee to take action on each draft resolution at the plenary meeting following that at which the draft resolution was introduced, or at a later date. Any anticipated action on draft resolutions or decisions will be announced in advance, including in the Journal and on the website of the Committee. It is the practice of the Sixth Committee to strive for the adoption of draft resolutions without taking a vote. Conference resources available
